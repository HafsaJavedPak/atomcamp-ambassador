Your Role & Responsibilities

As a Campus Ambassador, you are the official face of Atomcamp at your university. Here's everything the role entails.

🌱 Community Building

Create a tech learning culture on campus. Engage students interested in AI, Data, Web, Digital Marketing and tech skills.

📣 Promotion & Content

Promote Atomcamp bootcamps, workshops, and programs. Create posts, reels, or testimonials that reach your peer network.

🎤 Event Organization

Organize at least 1 workshop per month — trainings, workshops, study circles, and awareness sessions.

🤝 Student Support

Guide students about bootcamps and learning paths. Answer basic questions about Atomcamp offerings and scholarships.

Program Duration

The Campus Ambassador program runs for 1 year. Your journey includes orientation, monthly activities, quarterly training, and a final evaluation with graduation certificates.

Monthly Mandatory Activities

Every ambassador must complete 3 activities per month across the following three pillars.

📢 1 · Promotion Activity

Share Atomcamp bootcamp posts · Promote scholarships · Create Instagram/LinkedIn content · Share student success stories

✍️ 2 · Content Creation

Reels promoting Atomcamp courses · Educational videos · Technical articles · Regular LinkedIn posts (minimum 1/month)

👥 3 · Community Growth

Add students to Atomcamp community groups · Recruit new learners · Conduct surveys about learning interests (questionnaire provided by Atomcamp)

Mandatory Minimums

Failing to complete all 3 activity types in a month affects your Activity & Consistency score (15% of total KPI). Consistency over perfection — a small effort every month beats one big sprint.

Community Building Playbook

Your goal is to become the "go-to tech person" on campus. Here is how you build a movement, not just a group chat.

The "Seed" Strategy

Don't just blast links. Find the 5 most active students in your department. Pitch them the vision of a "Data & AI Hub" on campus. These are your Champions. They will help you moderate groups and spread the word organically.

Engagement Tactics

Weekly Tool Showcase

Share one AI tool (e.g., Perplexity, Claude, Cursor) every Tuesday to keep the group active and curious.

The Quiz Method

Run a weekly poll on "Data Science myths" to spark debate and learning — quick, low-effort, high-engagement.

Handling Silent Groups

If your WhatsApp group goes quiet, ask a question. Example: "What's the #1 skill you're worried about after graduating?" A good question beats ten announcements.

Content Strategy & Tone

Content is how you scale your influence beyond your immediate circle. Stay in these buckets and you'll never run dry.

The Content Buckets

Behind the Scenes

You studying an Atomcamp course or prepping for an event. Show the process, not just the result.

Success Story

Sharing a peer who got a job or landed a skill after an AI bootcamp. Real stories convert.

Tech Value

Tips on Prompt Engineering, Python basics, or AI tools. Educational content builds authority.

Sample Post Hooks

"Universities teach us 2010 tech. Atomcamp teaches us 2026 skills. Here's what I learned this week..."

"Prompt Engineering isn't a hobby — it's a career baseline. I'm hosting a session next Friday..."

Quarterly Content Requirement

Every ambassador must publish one technical article per quarter. Choose a topic within the Atomcamp scope (AI, data, Python, digital skills, career tips). This counts toward your Content & Promotion KPI.

The Event Lifecycle

Events are the Super-KPI. One great event can hit all your monthly targets in a single day.

Event Checkpoint Checklist

  • 14 Days Before: Finalize topic (e.g. "Agentic AI 101") and lock in the date.
  • 10 Days Before: Send promo graphics to Atomcamp CM for review and approval.
  • 7 Days Before: Launch registration form and begin the "hype" posts.
  • 1 Day Before: Send "Final Reminder" to all registrants via WhatsApp and email.
  • Event Day: TAKE PHOTOS. A group selfie is mandatory for the monthly report.
  • Post-Event: Send a "Thank You" note + Atomcamp referral link to all attendees.

Target Attendance

🖥️ Online Sessions

Target: 30–50 participants per session

🏫 Physical Sessions

Target: 10–20 participants per session

🤝 Collaborative Events

When co-organized with other ambassadors, attendance targets scale proportionally upward.

Student FAQ Guide

Students will ask you these questions. Use these verified answers to maintain authority and accuracy.

"Are Atomcamp bootcamps only for CS students?"
Not at all! Atomcamp offers bootcamps for Digital Marketing, HR Analytics, and Data Analytics specifically designed for non-techies. AI skills are for everyone — regardless of your degree.
"Is there a certificate at the end?"
Yes. Every bootcamp comes with a verified certificate and industry-aligned projects you can add directly to your portfolio.
"How long are the bootcamps?"
Duration varies by program. Students should check the Atomcamp website or ask you directly — always refer them to the official pages for the most current information.
"Do I need prior experience to join?"
Most bootcamps are beginner-friendly. Atomcamp is designed to take students from zero to job-ready, so prior experience isn't a requirement for most tracks.

Troubleshooting & Support

Running a program for the first time is hard. Here's how to fix the most common issues quickly.

📉 Low Event Signups?

Partner with an existing university society (CS Society, IEEE Chapter, ACM). Let them take co-credit for the event. Their audience + your content = full room.

📱 Zero Social Engagement?

Tag official Atomcamp handles in every post. Share your post link in the Ambassador WhatsApp group so everyone can boost it together.

💤 Silent WhatsApp Group?

Ask a question. Try: "What's the #1 skill you're worried about after graduating?" Always sparks conversation.

🤔 Unsure About Something?

Always escalate to your Community Manager before giving uncertain answers to students. Never guess about bootcamp details — accuracy matters.
